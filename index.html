Understanding Passion Score

The Passion Score helps identify posts where authors invested significant effort writing detailed content, but received minimal community engagement.

Passion Score = (Text Length Score) / (Engagement + 1)

Where:
• Text Length Score = min(text_length / 500, 10)
• Engagement = votes + (comments × 2)

A higher score indicates more "overlooked" content – posts with substantial text but low engagement.

Example: A 2,500-character post with only 3 votes and 1 comment would have a high passion score (indicating high effort, low recognition), while a 500-character post with 50 votes would have a low score.

This metric helps surface thoughtful, detailed contributions that may have been posted at the wrong time or simply missed by the community.
